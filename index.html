A comprehensive compilation of Plant Pests of the Middle East (PPME) is long overdue.
Agriculture in the Middle East has much in common with agriculture in many other parts of the world, but with many distinct characteristics. The same can be said for pests in this region. Some have originated or are found in other parts of the world, while others are endemic to the Middle East. Considerable information of the different pest species, their relative importance, and on measures taken to control them, has accumulated in this region, with agriculture intensifying and changing its focus. The agricultural community must comply with environmental considerations and still maintain a proper balance between crop production and the economics of control. Understanding pest-pest and pest-plant interactions in the agro-ecosystems of the Middle East and their effects on specific crops, is essential for maintaining good agricultural practice and balancing between the needs of the agricultural community and demands of the general public, from the economic as well as the environmental points of view.

Accessibility of information is a major concern: Published books and scientific journals are costly whereas unpublished reports are usually unavailable. Information printed in books is often outdated almost from the time of publication and is not updated until new editions appear, if at all. Commercial considerations of publishing, such as the costs of color photographs and graphic art, limit their incorporation into conventional books. These reservations do not apply to this online "virtual" publication. PPME contains invited chapters and items. It is intended for the undergraduate student in agriculture, for the postgraduate student or researcher in plant protection, but is also suitable for extension workers, informed farmers and non-professional readers who are interested in the subject. To assist the reader, "highlighted" terms and concepts are summarized in the glossary.

Due to the use of different vernacular names that are applied to the various pests and natural enemies that are covered in PPME, the Index is arranged according to the scientific names of these arthropods, but their more common names are included whenever appropriate.

We envision PPME as being an "open-ended" text, with chapters to be placed on the internet site after being reviewed, accepted and edited. These chapters will be modified and updated if and when new information becomes available: The composing and editing of PPME is an ongoing process.
