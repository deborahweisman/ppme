Pentalonia nigronervosa

Pentalonia nigronervosa Coquerel

Taxonomic placing: Insecta, Hemiptera, Sternorrhyncha, Aphidoidea, Aphididae.

Common name: Banana aphid.

Geographical distribution: This pest occurs throughout al tropical and subtropical parts of the world, having also invaded greenhouses in Europe and North America. Commonwealth Institute of Entomology Map #242, 1968.

Host plants: Banana (Musa acuminata) and several monocotyledonous ornamentals (e.g. Dieffenbachia).

Economic importance: The banana aphid is a pest of banana, occurring in large colonies all over the plant, including its subterranean parts. The aphid produces much honeydew that is colonized by disfiguring sooty mold fungi. Large numbers of ants attend the pest and may hinder the activity of its natural enemies. Aphid infestation reduces banana vigor, retards its development and brings about culling of contaminated fruit. Abroad P. nigronervosa vectors the “bunchy top” virus disease of bananas.

Morphology: The body of apterous and alate females, including the siphunculi and cauda, is dark-red to black. The body of both female forms is 1.3 -1.7 mm long.

Life cycle: In the Middle East the banana aphid reproduces by viviparous parthenogenesis throughout the year. It is most active during autumn and winter, when the pest migrates from the subterranean parts of the banana to its upper parts, including the heart leaves. As temperatures rise the aphids move back into the soil, where they remain till next autumn.

Management

Cultural control: Banana fruit infestations of the bunches can be reduced by covering them with polyethylene bags.

Chemical control: In the Middle East this aphid is usually a minor pest, which may be controlled with organophosphates. Another option is applying pesticides against associated ants, which transport the aphids from site to site.

Biological control: The natural enemies of this pest have no been studied in the Middle East.
