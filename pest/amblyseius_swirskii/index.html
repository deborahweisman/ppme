Amblyseius swirskii

Amblyseius swirskii Athias-Henriot

Taxonomic placing: Acari, Mesostigmata, Phytoseiidae.

Common name: None.

Geographical distribution: Middle East.

Host plants: Many indigenous and introduced plants.

Morphology: Dorsum about 0.3-0.4 mm in length, almost smooth, with a few reticulations, and 17 pairs of nude setae plus two pairs of lateral setae. The sternal plate bears three pairs of setae, and there are three pairs of preanal setae.

Economic importance: Amblyseius swirskii is the dominant summer phytoseiid predator in citrus groves along the coastal plain of Israel, where it is possibly one of the main natural enemies of the citrus rust mite, Phyllocoptruta oleivora (Ashmead). More recently _A. swirskii _has been developed by the Dutch company Koppert B.V. as a natural enemy of thrips (Thysanoptera), and whiteflies (Aleyrodidae). By late 2006 more than one billion predators, mostly within slow-release bags, known as Swirski-Mite Plus, have been sold.

Life history:The female mite required less than one week to complete a generation at 27°C, produced 44 eggs when feeding on an eriophyid mite and lived about four weeks. The predator also feeds on other prey, including many pest mites, thrips, whiteflies, the eggs of moths (Lepidoptera) and the crawlers of scale insects (Coccoidea), as well as on pollen. Amblyseius swirskii has no winter diapause.
